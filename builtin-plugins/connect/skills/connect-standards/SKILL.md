# Microsoft Connect — writing standards

Use these standards whenever drafting or improving a Connect self-assessment.

## What a Connect is

A structured self-assessment **document + two-way conversation** between employee and
manager. Captures the **what** (impact delivered), the **how** (behaviors/culture), and
**goals + growth plan**. Required at least twice per fiscal year.

## Required structure

1. **Impact — What did you deliver?** results + measurable business/customer outcomes
2. **Impact — How did you deliver it?** how you worked / interacted / led (Microsoft culture)
3. **Reflection & growth** — honest about gaps, missed opportunities, lessons, feedback acted on
4. **Goals (SMART)** — bold but achievable; state why + impact; aligned to priorities (security, quality, AI)
5. **Growth plan** — specific skills, actions, measurable indicators, timeline

Alt framing: My Current Role (summary/responsibilities/priorities) → My Reflection
(accomplishments / opportunities for growth) → Focused Growth Opportunity → Action Plan
and Timeline. Often a ~3-paragraph self-assessment.

## Writing standards

- **Content:** outcomes > activities. Every claim ties Action → Scale → Measurable impact → Business outcome.
- **Length:** short, high-signal. 1–2 strong examples, NOT exhaustive lists. "Don't write a book."
- **Tone:** professional, direct, evidence-based, growth mindset (own gaps, no excuses).
- **Style:** strong verbs (lead, drive, collaborate, improve, leverage, build); concise; avoid vague narrative.
- **Behaviors:** show the impact of behaviors, not just "attended events".

## Quality bar

- BAD:  "Led meetings and collaborated with stakeholders."
- GOOD: "Increased stakeholder satisfaction 30% by establishing a cross-team collaboration
        cadence and aligning priorities early."
- Pattern: "Delivered IoT offering to GA — 12 projects, 126 feedback items, +20% nominations."

## AI-usage policy (guardrail)

- AI is a **drafting assistant, not the author**. Final content must be **written in the
  employee's own voice** and **manually reviewed + edited**.
- **Prohibited:** using AI output as the final submission; using AI to assess performance.
- The draft must always be framed as a *draft to personalize*; keep the human in control and
  never present the output as an authoritative rating.
